How I Work

The approach Wire 3's
6-step framework deserves.

01

Understand the customer before building anything

Who are they? What pain brought them here, and what would make them leave? I've run formal VOC programs at Charter and Pall Water, and I bring that customer-first discipline to every lifecycle decision. The program follows the insight — never the other way around.

02

Know your differentiation — and build messaging around it

Understanding the customer is half the equation. The other half is knowing what your solution uniquely solves and why it's the right answer for them specifically. That clarity is what makes lifecycle communication earn attention instead of just filling an inbox.

03

Hypothesize, test, scale — never skip the middle step

Kaizen and 5 Whys methodology means every program starts with a clear objective and a defined measurement plan. I've run controlled testing cycles for years — evaluated before scaled, every time.

04

Read the signals before churn happens

Segmented by engagement timelines and behavioral indicators — not waiting for the cancel signal. At Ingenovis I rebuilt re-engagement strategy around proactive outreach at defined thresholds, not reactive damage control after the fact.

At Pall Water, we used root-cause analysis to identify why onboarding projections underperformed — evaluate, hypothesize, test, review, refine. That's not a new process for me. It's a standard I'm committed to, every time."
